# Style DNA

## In One Sentence

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, plenty of white space, restrained, bizarre, product sketch feel, handwritten English feel, clear structure but not an instruction manual.

It should look like an explanatory sketch casually drawn on a blank piece of paper by someone who has worked in AI, product, design, or dev tools for a long time.

## Must Haves

- 16:9 horizontal in-text illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage paper feel.
- Primarily black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector, no heavy strokes.
- Plenty of white space: the main subject takes up about 40%-60% of the screen, leaving at least 35% blank space, ideally with a large, quiet empty area.
- Sparse handwritten English annotations: at most 5-8 places, 1-3 words per place ideally.
- One image only explains one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- The structure should be expressed naturally; do not write the structure type name on the image.

## Colors

- **Black:** Main line art, characters, outlines, structure, main text, main objects.
- **Red:** Key annotations, problems, emotional points, critical reminders, results.
- **Orange:** Main flows, paths, arrows, automation flow, movement relationships from A to B.
- **Blue:** Supplementary explanations, mental states, system states, secondary explanations, AI/assistant/automation prompts.

Blue is not required for every image. Color usage must be restrained; less is more.

## Absolutely Do Not

- No commercial illustrations.
- No PPT infographics.
- No formal flowcharts.
- No course slides.
- No cute cartoon posters.
- No children's illustrations.
- No complex architecture diagrams.
- No exquisite flat illustrations.
- No sci-fi/tech UI.
- No realistic App screenshots.
- No complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Do not try to explain every single node perfectly.
- Do not write type titles like "Workflow Flowchart / System Architecture Diagram / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap" in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic Direction

It should be bizarre, creative, interesting, simple, clean, and wildly imaginative.

It should not be cute, childish, overly complex, or rigid.
