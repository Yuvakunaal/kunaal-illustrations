# Reference Characters IP

## Character Definition

The "Reference Boy" and "Reference Girl" are the fixed visual IPs for Kunaal's Illustrations.

If the prompt explicitly requests a person, or if the scene requires a human element, one of these characters must appear in the image. They are not mascots, stickers, or cute decorations, but absurd workers seriously participating in the system's operation. If the prompt is just about an animal or object without people, do not include them.

## Reference Boy Appearance

A minimalist digital doodle of a young boy based on the character style of references/images/10-boy.png: 
- Short black hair.
- Simple dot eyes and a neutral expression (unless the prompt asks for a specific emotion).
- Wearing a plain light blue t-shirt and light grey pants.

## Reference Girl Appearance

A minimalist, clean vector line art illustration of a cute young girl, based on the character style of references/images/09-girl.png:
- She has dot eyes and a simple concentrated expression.
- Her hair is tied in a high ponytail.
- She is wearing a pale yellow t-shirt and grey pants.
- Must be drawn in the wobbly line-art style as the rest of the image.

## Multiple Characters Rule

If the scene requires multiple instances of the Boy or the Girl (e.g., "three boys playing" or "a group of girls"), **do not draw exact clones**. 
You must randomly vary their t-shirt colors, and optionally make slight adjustments to their heights or minor details, so that they look like distinct individuals while strictly maintaining the same minimalist line-art style.

## Personality

- Very serious, but what they are doing is a bit absurd.
- Like a low-profile system operator.
- Deadpan humor, no attempts to be cute.
- Slightly clumsy, but not stupid.
- Looks like they are actually responsible for a task in a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Duties

Let the character undertake the core action:

- Carrying materials.
- Pulling lines to gather information sources.
- Getting stuck in a breakpoint.
- Operating the "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Turning into a filtering funnel.
- Slicing a "material fish."
- Stamping the continuation script.
- Pulling the continuation path.
- Holding a warning sign to watch out for pitfalls.
- Reaching out of a hole but failing to catch content.
- Moving bricks, building bridges, opening doors, sorting, or recording on the side.

## Prohibitions

- Do not draw the character as an overly cute mascot.
- Do not draw them as a children's cartoon character or highly realistic/anime human.
- Do not give the character complex clothing, emojis, or shiny eyes.
- Do not let the character just stand in the corner watching.
- Do not let the character steal the structural expression of the image.
- Do not draw the character too commercially, too round, or too exquisite.

## Judgment Criteria

If you remove the character and the core metaphor of the image still completely holds up, it means they are too decorative; you need to rewrite the prompt so the character becomes the subject of the action.
